🚨 Emergency & CPR Guide

Step-by-step help for a water emergency or someone in distress. Tap 🔊 so your hands stay free, or turn on 🎤 voice control and say "next" / "back" / "repeat".

📞 If this is happening right now: send someone to call 911 (local emergency services) immediately — do this before or while you start helping. This guide is a quick reference, not a substitute for certified CPR/first-aid training. Get certified through the Red Cross or an equivalent local provider.
🌊 Water Rescue
❤️ CPR Steps
Step 1 of 7
This guide covers general layperson CPR guidance for adults. It does not cover infant/child CPR, which uses different techniques. If the person recovers and seems fine, they still need to see a doctor — water in the lungs can cause serious problems hours later even after someone seems okay. Get certified: a hands-on Red Cross (or equivalent) CPR/first-aid course takes a few hours and is the real preparation — this page is only a memory-jogger in the moment.
